Summary

Over a decade of professional experience with visual and web design while travelling extensively around the world and living in Brazil, Tunisia, Italy, England and the United States. Currently based in New York City.

I speak and write in English and Portuguese fluently, in Italian well, and in French and Spanish in a somewhat amusing way. Using these together with visual and programming languages I have managed to:

  • Raise funding for international organizations by completing branding and user experience design projects in digital and print media.
  • Save companies time and money with good web applications and thoughtful design.
  • Help groups and individuals with hands-on design, coding, editing, brainstorming and wine tasting.

I enjoy learning and absorbing information from different fields.

Education ↑ back to top

I learned to work with computers with computer games. I learned graphic design from books, posters and films by some of my favorite designers like Milton Glaser, Saul Bass and Paul Rand. I learned HTML and CSS by viewing the source of websites I liked, searching with Google and with Jeffrey Zeldman’s Designing With Web Standards. I learned user experience, usability and accessibility principles from a book called The Cluetrain Manifesto and from Jacob Nielsen’s Alertbox column. I learned photography by taking and looking at photographs. I learned to cook and to eat in Italy. I’ve been learning some Python and enjoyed meeting Alex Martelli in Florence in 2013.

My traditional education occurred at:

Skills, Tools & Interests ↑ back to top

Web development: I like HTML, CSS, JavaScript, web standards and open source software. If a tool is the right one for the job I’ll use it.

User experience design, usability, accessibility: I like fast, simple interfaces and good writing over excessive style.

Photography: I work with Leica rangefinder cameras and Fuji and Ricoh compacts. I want a better, more affordable camera for photography students and better editing software.

Software: I spend most of my time in Photoshop, Sketch, Illustrator, Lightroom, InDesign and in Textmate and a Terminal prompt. I also have some experience with Python and Django, PHP, Wordpress, Drupal and Textpattern, Ruby and Jekyll, Git and Github, Apache and Nginx, a little MySQL and PostgreSQL and a bit of Unix system administration. Here’s the software I’m using in 2015.

Interests: Communication, information, photography, web, travel, open source & open internet, philosophy, typography, art history, nature, astronomy, computer simulations, music, (blues guitar, jazz, classical), vintage aeroplanes, basketball.
