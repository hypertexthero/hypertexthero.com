Summary

I enjoy learning and absorbing information from different fields to help solve problems, keep my brain whirring and, with any luck, pass on knowledge to future generations.

With over a decade of professional graphic and web design experience while travelling extensively around the world and living in Brazil, Tunisia, Italy, England and the United States I have learned to speak and write in: English and Portuguese fluently, Italian well, French and Spanish in a somewhat amusing way. Using these together with visual and programming languages I have managed to:

  • Increase funding for and cooperation between international organizations.
  • Save people time and make them happier when using computers.
  • Help groups and individuals with hands-on design, coding, editing, brainstorming and critique.

I’m based in and ♥ New York City.

Education ↑ back to top

I learned to work with computers with computer games. I learned graphic design from books, interfaces, posters and films by designers like Milton Glaser, Susan Kare, Robert Bringhurst and Saul Bass. I learned HTML and CSS by viewing the source of websites, searching with Google and reading Jeffrey Zeldman’s Designing With Web Standards. I learned user experience, usability and accessibility principles from The Cluetrain Manifesto, Don’t Make Me Think and Jacob Nielsen’s Alertbox column. I learned photography by taking and looking at photographs. I learned to cook and to eat in Italy. I’ve been learning some Python and enjoyed meeting Alex Martelli in Florence in 2013.
