Summary

Over a decade of professional visual and web design experience while travelling extensively around the world and living in Brazil, Tunisia, Italy, England and the United States. Currently based in New York City.

I speak and write in English and Portuguese fluently, in Italian well, and in French and Spanish in a somewhat amusing way. Using these together with visual and programming languages I have managed to:

  • Increase funding for and cooperation between international organizations through branding and user experience design projects in digital and print media.
  • Save people time and make them happier when using computers through useful web applications and thoughtful interface design.
  • Help groups and individuals with hands-on design, coding, editing, brainstorming and critique.

I enjoy learning and absorbing information from different fields to solve problems and keep my brain whirring.

Education ↑ back to top

I learned to work with computers with computer games. I learned graphic design from books, interfaces, posters and films by designers like Milton Glaser, Susan Kare and Saul Bass. I learned HTML and CSS by viewing the source of websites, searching with Google and reading Jeffrey Zeldman’s Designing With Web Standards. I learned user experience, usability and accessibility principles from The Cluetrain Manifesto, Don’t Make Me Think and Jacob Nielsen’s Alertbox column. I learned photography by taking and looking at photographs. I learned to cook and to eat in Italy. I’ve been learning some Python and enjoyed meeting Alex Martelli in Florence in 2013.
