Agile Notes

Friday, February 15 2013

I found my notes made at a presentation by Andrea Provaglio to the IT team at FAO back in November 2011 (thanks, Erik!). Here they are for posterity:

Agile (xtreme programming, scrum, etc) vs. Waterfall development vs. Big Upfront Design:

  • 50 Years ago, a mistake was made in software development: “building software like building physical objects” …industrial revolution…factories…
  • Software not predictable, not deterministic…need to learn on the go
  • Writing software: make hypothesis, try & test, decide what to do next
  • Over time conditions, specifications, the world, changes
  • Tighter feedback loop is needed, 2 week cycles, short iterations
  • Working with knowledge. Sharing important networks. Gestalt theory: whole > sum of parts

Agile Manifesto

  • Individuals and interactions more important than processes and tools1
  • Working software is more important than documentation
  • Customer collaboration over contract negotiation2
  • Responding to change is more important than following a plan

How to be agile in an un-agile organization?

  • Start doing it yourself & in your sphere of influence (subvert)
  • IBM guy, agile implemented in unofficial internal IRC tool — started evolving very quickly — quick feedback & progress…
  • Sell flexibility, avoid scope and budget



                scope
                  /\  
                 /  \ 
                /    \
     FLEXIBILITY –––––– budget

  • 4 ways:
    • Do it without telling anyone
    • “Either I deliver, or I don’t get paid”
    • Rolling contracts: 12 months long, 12 one month iterations, paid at end of each iteration
    • Percentage up front (rolling contract)

Retrospectives

  • What have we done that worked?
  • What can we improve? (not “what have we done wrong?”)

Software not deterministic…need feedback loops…water in shower, hose is short, feedback is quick.

Standup meeting = scrum (standing to keep it short)

  • What did I do yesterday?
  • What do I want to do today?
  • What is stopping me from doing my work?

Cross-functional Teams

Agile team = 5–9 people. More? Divide teams.
Agile coach = experience, objectivity.

  • Everyone in same team, one single entity
  • Engines: There is a best RPM rate, go over and you stress the engine == stress to the team & quality degrades
  • Stressing knowledge workers (any workers) doesn’t work

Refactoring

  • Make better without changing behaviour
  • Make design more simple

Simplicity is the act of maximizing work that is NOT done.

Continuous Integration

  • Auto-commit & test
  • Get feedback

1 Who has a hard time with agile? Middle management.

2 Contracts are for figuring out what to do if things go bad.
