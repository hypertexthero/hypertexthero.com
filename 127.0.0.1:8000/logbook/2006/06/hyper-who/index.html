About

Thursday, June 29 2006

h1. Hyper-who?

Hypertexthero is a small design studio specializing in usable, “high quality web and graphic design”:/. I’m Simon Griffee, the pilot!

I learned to use computers while hacking MS/DOS to make games run in the early 1990s.[1] After completing a Creative Advertising Design degree from Bournemouth University in 1999, I realized that the majority of things are not very good at all and certainly not worth the effort spent to convince other people to buy them.

I taught myself graphic design and HTML and worked in small internet start-ups and large organizations. I am passionate about the internet which I believe to be one of humanity’s greatest inventions.

I am most delighted when doing worthwhile design work, when being with great art or people, when “photographing (Photographs by Simon Griffee.)”:http://simongriffee.com, when in the flow. I record interesting sites I come across in my internet travels in the “Logbook”:/logbook/.

Here’s “my résumé”:/cv/, some of my “code”:http://github.com/hypertexthero and my “contact details”:/contact/.

After a short time I decided that commuting to and spending every week day in an office were not what I wanted to live for. Rather, I wanted to work more comfortably and travel more often, so Hypertexthero came to be.

I am passionate about the internet which I believe to be one of humanity’s greatest inventions. h3. Why Hire Me

You need a modern website that looks good and is simple to use. You appreciate elegant graphic design that lets your audience hear your voice. I am an experienced designer known for “idiosyncratic work of high quality and effect (See a list of selected work.)”:/work/. I am “trained in advertising (BA in Creative Advertising from Bournemouth University.)”:/cv#bournemouth but don’t believe all things are worth selling. I believe that content should determine the technology, not the other way around. I am “a member (The Guild of Accessible Web Designers - Members Information - Simon Griffee.)”:http://www.gawds.org/showmember.php?memberid=1218 of the “Guild of Accessible Web Designers (GAWDS.org)”:http://www.gawds.org. I like to work on projects that “do no harm (Milton Glaser.)”:http://www.miltonglaser.com/pages/milton/essays/es3.html. I start with pencil and paper. I believe in technology working for people, not people working for machines. I think “web standards (Find out why web standards are important at the Web Standards Project website.)”:http://www.webstandards.org/about/ are “good for business(The business value of web standards.)”:http://www.adaptivepath.com/publications/essays/archives/000266.php and for “us all (The architects of HTML saw we were going digital and sat back and took a long view. A very long view.)”:http://www.thenoodleincident.com/tutorials/box_lesson/why.html. I believe in working to live, not living to work.

h3. History

Hypertexthero launched in May of 2005 when I left my home in “Formello (Sito internet di Formello.)”:http://www.lcnet.it/reticiviche/formello/formello.html, Italy to try living as a nomadic designer. I’m happy to say I’ve managed so far.
